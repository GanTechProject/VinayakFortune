## 1. Purpose & Scope

This document is the contract for the **sales motion**. It governs:

- How inbound and outbound leads are qualified.
- The messaging framework per persona.
- The discovery → demo → proposal flow.
- Objection handling.
- Hand-off to customer success.

It does not cover marketing (which is a separate function) or pricing (Document 31).

## 2. Sales principles

1. **Value first.** Every conversation starts with the customer's problem, not our product.
2. **Evidence-led.** Every claim we make is backed by a real artifact we can show.
3. **Honest qualification.** If we're not the right fit, we say so and refer.
4. **Multi-thread.** We always build a coalition, not a single relationship.
5. **Persistence with respect.** Follow-up is professional, never pushy.

## 3. Personas & buying centers

### 3.1 Primary buyers (per PRD §5)

- **P-1: Indie Founder** — Solo, self-serve, no sales touch.
- **P-2: Corporate Innovator** — Team/Enterprise; champion + economic buyer + procurement.
- **P-3: Early-Stage Investor** — Team; champion + ops/admin.
- **P-4: Consultant** — Team; champion + practice lead.

### 3.2 Buying centers (Enterprise)

| Role | Title (typical) | Cares about |
|---|---|---|
| Champion | Director, Innovation | Discovery speed, evidence quality |
| Economic buyer | VP Strategy / Head of Corp Dev | ROI, defensibility |
| Technical evaluator | Data / IT / Security | Security, integration, data residency |
| Procurement | Procurement Manager | Contract terms, vendor risk |
| Legal | Counsel | DPA, MSA, IP |
| End users | Analysts, researchers | Day-to-day experience |

We must build a coalition of at least 3 of these before close.

## 4. Messaging framework

### 4.1 Core message

> **VentureMiner AI turns market signals into validated, monetizable SaaS opportunities — in hours, not quarters.**

### 4.2 Per persona

| Persona | Hook | Proof |
|---|---|---|
| Indie founder | "Spend time building, not researching." | Time-to-first-report: 45 min |
| Corp innovator | "Quarterly board reports, in days, not months." | Sample board report (anonymized) |
| Investor | "Source off-market; write memos in 90s." | Sample deal memo |
| Consultant | "Deliver 3x more engagements per quarter." | Sample client report (white-label) |

### 4.3 Differentiators

- Multi-agent research depth.
- Evidence discipline (every claim cited).
- Editable rubric (transparent scoring).
- Designed for operators, not just researchers.

### 4.4 Anti-messaging (never claim)

- "100% accurate" (we are explicit about confidence).
- "Replaces your analyst" (we augment, not replace).
- "Magic" (we are rigorous, not magical).

## 5. Lead lifecycle

```
MQL → SAL → SQL → Demo → Proposal → Negotiation → Closed-Won → Hand-off to CS
```

- **MQL** (Marketing Qualified Lead) — sign-up, content download, event.
- **SAL** (Sales Accepted Lead) — BDR confirms fit.
- **SQL** (Sales Qualified Lead) — BDR/AE schedules discovery.
- **Demo** — first product demo.
- **Proposal** — written proposal + scope + price.
- **Negotiation** — legal/procurement.
- **Closed-Won** — contract signed.
- **Hand-off** — to CS.

## 6. Qualification (MEDDPICC)

- **M**etrics — what does success look like in dollars / hours?
- **E**conomic buyer — who signs?
- **D**ecision criteria — what does the eval look like?
- **D**ecision process — what's the steps and timeline?
- **P**aper process — legal, procurement, security review.
- **I**mplication of pain — what happens if they do nothing?
- **C**hampion — who's selling internally for us?
- **C**ompetition — what else are they evaluating?

We use MEDDPICC to qualify; **no discovery without a champion identified**.

## 7. Discovery calls

### 7.1 Structure (45 min)

1. **5 min** — rapport + agenda.
2. **15 min** — their problem (open-ended, listen).
3. **10 min** — current process + pain.
4. **10 min** — what success looks like + stakeholders.
5. **5 min** — next step (demo, security review, etc.).

### 7.2 Discovery questions (per persona)

**Indie founder (Solo — usually self-serve, no live call):** N/A.

**Corp innovator (Enterprise):**

- "How do you identify adjacencies today?"
- "How long does a board report take today?"
- "Who consumes the output, and what's the cadence?"
- "What's the cost of a missed opportunity?"

**Investor (Team):**

- "How do you source off-market today?"
- "How long does a deal memo take?"
- "How many meetings per sourced opportunity?"

**Consultant (Team):**

- "How do you scope an engagement today?"
- "What does a client deliverable look like?"
- "How many concurrent engagements can your team run?"

## 8. Demos

### 8.1 Principles

- **Their data, their problem.** Use a relevant seed in the demo.
- **Live, not slides.** Live product > 50 slides.
- **Cited evidence.** Show the citation panel — that's our differentiator.
- **Their stakeholders.** Tailor to who's on the call.

### 8.2 Demo flow (30 min)

1. **3 min** — context, what we'll show.
2. **5 min** — discovery in their industry.
3. **7 min** — validation on a candidate opportunity.
4. **5 min** — scoring + custom rubric.
5. **7 min** — report (one-page brief + full report preview).
6. **3 min** — Q&A + next step.

## 9. Objection handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We already use ChatGPT/Perplexity" | "Those are general-purpose; we're domain-tuned for venture intelligence. Let me show the difference on a real example." |
| "Too expensive" | "Compare to the cost of one analyst FTE; we're a fraction. And we don't replace — we amplify." |
| "Data privacy" | "SOC 2 in progress; per-tenant DEKs; data residency options; DPA available. I'll send the security one-pager." |
| "Hallucination" | "Every claim is cited. The verifier audits citations. Here's the citation panel in a live report." |
| "We need a pilot first" | "Great — we have a 3-month pilot program with success criteria. Let me send the pilot scope." |
| "Procurement is slow" | "Standard 12-month MSA, custom DPA, vendor risk form on file. We can move quickly." |
| "Not the right time" | "Understood. Can I check back in 60 days? I'll send a relevant report in the meantime." |

## 10. Proposals & quoting

- **Standard proposal template** (PDF, 5 pages).
- **Includes:** problem, solution, scope, success criteria, timeline, price, terms.
- **SOW** for Enterprise; pre-approved by Legal.
- **Quote** via Salesforce CPQ; integrated with Stripe.
- **Procurement** form pre-filled for known customers.

## 11. Closing

- **Verbal close → written close → signed.** No signature without a verbal confirmation.
- **Procurement & legal** walked in parallel when ACV > $50k.
- **Security review** scheduled immediately; pre-filled questionnaire available.
- **Counter-offers** routed to Sales Lead + Deal Desk.

## 12. Hand-off to CS

Within 5 business days of close:

- **Kickoff call** scheduled.
- **Implementation plan** drafted.
- **Admin training** scheduled.
- **Success plan** co-authored.
- **Stakeholder map** transferred.
- **QBR cadence** established.

## 15. Plays (per stage)

### 15.1 Outbound — Corp innovator

- LinkedIn message → email → 5-touch sequence.
- Hook: a sample report in their industry.
- CTA: 20-min discovery.

### 15.2 Inbound demo request

- Acknowledge within 1h.
- Schedule within 24h.
- Pre-call: send relevant case study.
- Demo: their industry.

### 15.3 Champion enablement

- Co-author a one-pager for the economic buyer.
- Provide a board-ready artifact.

### 15.4 Procurement acceleration

- Pre-filled vendor risk form.
- Standard MSA with redlines pre-reviewed.
- Security one-pager + SOC 2 letter of intent.

### 15.5 Renewal motion

- T-90: value snapshot report.
- T-60: QBR with stakeholder expansion.
- T-30: renewal proposal.
